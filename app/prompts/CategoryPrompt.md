## Regulatory Framework

- **Primary Law:** Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006 (FSS Act)
- **Core Regulation:** FSS (Food Products Standards & Food Additives) Regulations, 2011
- **Specialised Regulation:** FSS (Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals, FSDU, FSMP, Functional Food & Novel Food) Regulations, 2022
- **Infant Nutrition:** FSS (Infant Nutrition) Regulations, 2020 + IMS Act, 1992
- **Fortification:** FSS (Fortification of Foods) Regulations, 2018
- **Non-Specified / Proprietary:** FSS (Approval for Non-Specified Food & Food Ingredients) Regulations, 2017
- **Labelling:** FSS (Labelling and Display) Regulations, 2020
- **International Reference:** Codex Alimentarius Standards (STAN 72, 156, 074, 180)

## Category 1 — Standard Food

**Definition:** Any food article for which standards (composition, quality, safety limits, labelling) have been prescribed under the FSS Act, 2006 and Regulations framed thereunder. A product meeting these prescribed standards is classified as Standard Food.

**Regulation:** FSS (Food Products Standards & Food Additives) Regulations, 2011 — Schedule I (Product Standards), Schedule II (Food Additives), Appendix A (Contaminants), Appendix B (Pesticide Residues)

### Key Sub-Categories & Standards

| Sub-Category | Key Standards / Parameters |
|---|---|
| Dairy & Dairy Products | Milk, Cream, Butter, Ghee, Paneer, Dahi, Chhena, Khoa, Cheese, Ice Cream, SMP, WMP, Condensed Milk — fat %, SNF %, compositional limits |
| Edible Oils & Fats | Groundnut, Mustard, Coconut, Refined oils, Vanaspati, Margarine, Shortening — FFA, iodine value, fatty acid profile |
| Cereals & Cereal Products | Atta, Maida, Suji, Besan, Noodles, Bread, Biscuits, Starch — moisture, protein, ash limits |
| Fruits & Vegetable Products | Jams, Jellies, Marmalade, Pickles, Chutneys, Juices, Squashes, Syrups — Brix, preservative limits |
| Sweetening Agents | Sugar, Jaggery, Honey, Glucose, Lactose, Fructose — purity, moisture, invert sugar; Saccharin limits |
| Beverages | Tea, Coffee, Cocoa, Mineral Water, Packaged Drinking Water, Carbonated Drinks, Energy Drinks — micro limits, TDS, chemical parameters |
| Spices & Condiments | Coriander, Turmeric, Chilli, Pepper, Cumin, Garam Masala — identity, purity, volatile oil, ash tests |
| Meat & Poultry | Fresh/frozen meat, poultry carcass, processed meat — microbial limits, additive standards, cold chain |
| Fish & Fishery Products | Fresh, frozen, dried, cured fish; shrimp; surimi — TVBN, histamine, microbial limits |
| Salt, Vinegar & Related | Iodised salt (≥15 ppm iodine at consumption), edible salt, acetic acid, vinegar |
| Confectionery & Chocolates | Chocolate (milk/dark/white), candy, chewing gum — cocoa butter %, sugar limits, additive permissions |
| Food Additives (Schedule II) | Approved colours, preservatives, antioxidants, emulsifiers with INS numbers and category-specific limits |

**Key Identifiers:** Schedule I · Schedule II Additives · Appendix A Contaminants · Appendix B Pesticide Limits

## Category 2 — Fortified Food

**Definition:** Standard food to which one or more essential nutrients (vitamins, minerals, amino acids, fatty acids) have been added to prevent or correct a demonstrated deficiency in the population, or to restore nutrients lost during processing. Must carry the '+F' logo.

**Regulation:** FSS (Fortification of Foods) Regulations, 2018 | FSS (Food Products Standards & Food Additives) First Amendment Regulations, 2021

### Key Sub-Categories & Standards

| Sub-Category | Fortification Level / Standard |
|---|---|
| Fortified Wheat Flour (Atta) | Iron 14–42 mg/kg, Folic Acid 1–3 mg/kg, Vitamin B12 0.75–2.25 μg/kg |
| Fortified Rice | Iron 28–42.5 mg/kg, Folic Acid 75–125 μg/100g, Vitamin B12 0.75–1.25 μg/100g; optional Vitamin A, Zinc |
| Fortified Edible Oil | Vitamin A 6–10 IU/g, Vitamin D minimum 1100 IU/kg |
| Fortified Milk | Vitamin A 270–450 μg/L, Vitamin D 63–125 IU/100 mL |
| Double Fortified Salt (DFS) | Iodine ≥15 ppm + Iron 1 mg/g (encapsulated ferrous fumarate) |
| +F Logo & Certification | Mandatory on front-of-pack; FSSAI certification required; annual third-party audit; batch-level compliance |
| Voluntary vs. Mandatory | Voluntary unless covered under government schemes (POSHAN, Mid-Day Meal) — then mandatory for procured lots |

**Key Identifiers:** +F Logo Mandatory · Schedule XI Nutrients · Voluntary Unless Govt. Scheme · Audit & Certification Required

## Category 3 — Proprietary Food

**Definition:** An article of food not standardised under the FSS Act/Regulations, but not unsafe. A novel/unique product formulated by a manufacturer with specific composition not covered in existing FSSAI standards. Prior FSSAI approval required before market launch. If any food additive is included, the applicable Food Category System (FCS) category determines permitted additives.

**Regulation:** FSS (Approval for Non-Specified Food & Food Ingredients) Regulations, 2017 | Regulation 2.12.1 of Food Products Standards Regulations, 2011 | FSSAI Food Category System (FCS) — based on Codex Alimentarius GSFA

### Key Sub-Categories & Standards

| FCS Category | Applicable Proprietary Products |
|---|---|
| Food Category System (FCS) Framework | FCS (Codex-based) governs additive permissions — product classified into FCS category; Schedule II additives permitted for that FCS category apply |
| FCS 01 — Dairy & Analogues | Flavoured milk, dairy blends, UHT variants, cheese spreads, dairy desserts, non-dairy milk analogues |
| FCS 02 — Fats & Oils | Fat-based spreads, composite fats, non-dairy creamers, low-fat variants |
| FCS 04 — Fruits & Vegetables | Mixed fruit/vegetable beverages, dried blends, coated snacks, functional fruit preparations |
| FCS 06 — Cereal-Based Products | Ready-to-eat breakfast cereals, muesli, granola bars, cereal-based snacks |
| FCS 07 — Bakery Products | Specialty breads, cakes, pastries with novel ingredients, gluten-free baked goods |
| FCS 12 — Sauces, Dips & Seasonings | Composite sauces, salad dressings, seasoning blends, functional condiments |
| FCS 14 — Beverages | Functional beverages, flavoured waters, plant-based drinks, botanical infusions |
| FSSAI Product Approval Process | Submit formulation, safety data, label, shelf-life data to FSSAI Central — product-specific approval granted before launch |
| Label Declaration | Must declare "Proprietary Food" on front-of-pack; full ingredient list; no misleading claims |

**Key Identifiers:** Non-Standardised Product · Prior FSSAI Approval Required · FCS Additive Framework Applies · Label: "Proprietary Food"

## Category 4 — Health Supplements

**Definition:** A product (other than tobacco) intended to supplement the diet, containing one or more dietary ingredients such as vitamins, minerals, amino acids, fatty acids, enzymes, probiotics, or botanical/herbal substances. Intended for oral use in dose form only. No therapeutic or disease claims permitted.

**Regulation:** FSS (Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals, FSDU, FSMP, Functional Food & Novel Food) Regulations, 2022 — Schedule I (Permitted Ingredients & Upper Safe Levels)

### Key Sub-Categories & Standards

| Standard Area | Details |
|---|---|
| Permitted Ingredient Categories | Vitamins, Minerals, Amino acids, Fatty acids, Enzymes, Probiotics, Herbal/botanical extracts — from Schedule I approved list only |
| Upper Safe Levels (USL) | Daily dose must not exceed USL specified in Schedule I of HS&N Regulations, 2022 for each ingredient |
| Permitted Dosage Forms | Tablets, capsules, softgels, powders, liquids, granules, lozenges — not in conventional food form |
| Labelling Requirements | "Health Supplement" on front-of-pack; "Not for medicinal use"; daily recommended intake; serving size; warning statements |
| Claim Restrictions | Only nutrient function and structure-function claims permitted. Disease claims strictly prohibited. Claims must be FSSAI pre-approved |
| Safety & Quality Standards | Microbial limits, heavy metals (Pb, Cd, Hg, As), pesticide residues, solvent residues — per Schedule to HS&N Regulations, 2022 |
| Manufacturing Requirements | GMP as per Schedule 2 of FSS (Licensing & Registration) Regulations; FSSAI Central licence mandatory |

**Key Identifiers:** Schedule I Permitted List · USL Compliance Mandatory · Dose Form Only · No Therapeutic/Disease Claims · FSSAI Central Licence

## Category 5 — Nutraceuticals

**Definition:** A product derived from food sources providing extra health benefits beyond basic nutritional value. May be in conventional food form or dose form. Includes dietary fibre, prebiotics, omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants, phytosterols, and other bioactive substances. Distinguished from health supplements in that they can be in food form and may have a role in prevention of chronic disease.

**Regulation:** FSS (Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals, FSDU, FSMP, Functional Food & Novel Food) Regulations, 2022 — Schedule II (Permitted Nutraceutical Ingredients & Specifications)

### Key Sub-Categories & Standards

| Ingredient Class | Specifications |
|---|---|
| Dietary Fibre | Inulin, FOS, Beta-Glucan, Psyllium husk, Pectin — source specifications, purity limits, daily dose guidance |
| Omega-3 Fatty Acids | EPA, DHA, ALA from fish oil or algal oil — peroxide value, anisidine value limits; dose caps per Schedule |
| Antioxidants / Carotenoids | Lycopene, Lutein, Zeaxanthin, Astaxanthin, Resveratrol, Coenzyme Q10 — source and daily intake per Schedule II |
| Phytosterols & Stanols | Plant sterol esters from vegetable oils — maximum 3 g/day; cholesterol-lowering claim if used |
| Amino Acids (Non-essential/Conditional) | Glutamine, Arginine, Taurine, Carnitine, Creatine — purity, daily intake limits in Schedule |
| Herbal / Botanical Extracts | Approved list only: Ashwagandha, Turmeric extract (Curcumin), Ginger extract, Brahmi — standardised extract specifications |
| Conventional Food or Dose Form | Can be incorporated into food (omega-3 bread, probiotic yoghurt) or sold in dose form — both regulated equally |
| Permitted Health Claims | Health claims and nutrient claims (not disease claims) — per FSSAI health claims framework |

**Key Identifiers:** Schedule II Ingredients · Food or Dose Form · Health Claims Permitted · No Disease Claims · Source Specifications Required

## Category 6 — Food for Special Dietary Use (FSDU)

**Definition:** Foods specially processed or formulated to satisfy particular dietary requirements due to a physical, physiological, metabolic, or disease condition — including foods for weight reduction, intolerance conditions (gluten, lactose), sports performance, and pregnant/lactating women. Must differ significantly in composition from ordinary food to meet those special needs.

**Regulation:** FSS (Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals, FSDU, FSMP, Functional Food & Novel Food) Regulations, 2022 — Schedule V (Compositional Standards for FSDU Sub-Categories)

### Key Sub-Categories & Standards

| Sub-Category | Compositional Standard |
|---|---|
| Gluten-Free Food | Gluten ≤20 ppm (labelled "Gluten-Free"); Very Low Gluten: 21–100 ppm; cross-contamination controls mandatory throughout manufacturing |
| Lactose-Free / Low-Lactose | Lactose-Free: ≤0.01 g/100 mL; Low-Lactose: ≤2 g/100 g — enzyme (lactase) treatment or alternative base required |
| Foods for Diabetics | Low Glycaemic Index; no added simple sugars (sucrose, glucose); approved polyols/non-caloric sweeteners; calorie declaration mandatory |
| Foods for Weight Control | Meal replacements: 200–400 kcal/serving, protein ≥25% of energy, full micronutrient profile per RDA; VLCD total ≤800 kcal/day |
| Sports / Active Nutrition | Protein supplements (whey, casein, soy, plant): protein %, amino acid profile (PDCAAS/DIAAS), banned substance testing per WADA |
| Foods for Pregnant & Lactating Women | Enhanced Folic acid, Iron, Calcium, DHA — within USL; not a substitute for medical nutrition advice |
| Vegan / Plant-Based Foods | No animal-derived ingredients; declaration on label; B12 fortification advisable; FSSAI may require certification |

**Key Identifiers:** Special Population Targeting · Schedule V Standards · Specific Criteria Per Sub-Category · Label: "Food for Special Dietary Use" · Cannot Replace Medical Advice

## Category 7 — Food for Special Medical Purpose (FSMP)

**Definition:** Food specially processed or formulated for dietary management of patients (including infants) who have limited, impaired, or disturbed capacity to take, digest, absorb, metabolise, or excrete ordinary foodstuffs, certain nutrients, or metabolites — and whose dietary management cannot be achieved by modification of normal diet alone. Must be used under medical supervision.

**Regulation:** FSS (Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals, FSDU, FSMP, Functional Food & Novel Food) Regulations, 2022 — Schedule VI | Codex Alimentarius STAN 180-1991

### Key Sub-Categories & Standards

| Sub-Category | Compositional / Clinical Standard |
|---|---|
| Complete Nutritional Formula (Sole-Source) | Sole source of nutrition — must meet full RDA for energy, protein, all micronutrients; e.g., tube-feeding formulas, total enteral nutrition (TEN) |
| Incomplete / Supplementary (Disease-Specific) | Covers partial nutrition for a specific condition — used alongside regular diet; must declare it is not a complete nutrition source |
| Renal Disease Formula | Low protein (0.6–0.8 g/kg/day), Low phosphorus (<800 mg/day), Low potassium — compositional limits per Schedule VI |
| Hepatic Disease Formula | High BCAA, Low AAA — Fischer Ratio optimised; ammonia management; low sodium |
| Metabolic Disorder Formulas | PKU: phenylalanine-free; MSUD: BCAA-free; Homocystinuria: methionine-free — must meet all other amino acid requirements |
| Oncology / Critical Care Formulas | High calorie (1.5–2 kcal/mL), High protein (≥20% energy), EPA-enriched, Glutamine, Arginine for immune modulation |
| Wound Healing Formulas | Arginine ≥5 g/day, Glutamine, Zinc ≥15 mg/day, Vitamin C ≥200 mg/day, Vitamin E — minimum levels per evidence base |
| Labelling & Distribution Restrictions | "For use under medical supervision only" — mandatory front-of-pack; not for general OTC retail; B2B/pharmacy/hospital channels |

**Key Identifiers:** Medical Supervision Mandatory · Schedule VI Standards · Sole or Supplemental Nutrition · Not OTC General Retail · Codex STAN 180-1991

## Category 8 — Prebiotic / Probiotic Food

**Definition:** Probiotic food contains defined, live microorganisms (≥10⁸ CFU/g or mL at point of consumption) that confer a health benefit on the host. Prebiotic food contains non-digestible ingredients selectively stimulating beneficial gut bacteria. Synbiotic foods combine both. Strains must be exclusively from the Schedule IV approved list.

**Regulation:** FSS (Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals, FSDU, FSMP, Functional Food & Novel Food) Regulations, 2022 — Schedule IV (Permitted Probiotic Microorganism Strains)

### Key Sub-Categories & Standards

| Standard Area | Details |
|---|---|
| Permitted Probiotic Strains (Schedule IV) | Lactobacillus acidophilus, L. rhamnosus GG, L. casei, L. plantarum, Bifidobacterium longum, B. infantis, B. lactis, B. bifidum, Streptococcus thermophilus, Saccharomyces boulardii — only listed strains permitted |
| Minimum Viable Count (MVC) | ≥10⁸ CFU/g or mL at point of consumption throughout stated shelf life — validated by stability studies |
| Strain Identity Requirements | Genus, species, and strain designation mandatory on label; molecular characterisation (16S rRNA or ITS sequencing) at manufacturing stage |
| Safety Assessment | GRAS / QPS status required; antibiotic resistance profiling; no virulence factors; no transferable resistance genes |
| Permitted Prebiotic Ingredients | FOS, Inulin, GOS, Lactulose, Resistant Starch, Long-chain Inulin, Pectin — dose and source specifications apply |
| Permitted Claims | Structure/function claims only: "supports gut health", "maintains intestinal flora balance", "helps improve digestion" — FSSAI pre-approved claim list |
| Storage & Viability | Temperature, humidity, packaging conditions ensuring viability must be declared; viability throughout shelf life demonstrated with data |

**Key Identifiers:** Schedule IV Strains Only · ≥10⁸ CFU/g at Consumption · Strain Designation on Label · Stability Data Required · No Disease Claims

## Category 9 — Infant Nutrition Food

**Definition:** Foods specifically formulated to meet nutritional requirements of infants (0–12 months) and young children (1–3 years), including infant formula, follow-up formula, growing-up milk, and complementary foods. Designed as a substitute or supplement to breast milk. Subject to strict compositional, safety, and marketing restrictions under both FSSAI and the IMS Act, 1992.

**Regulation:** FSS (Infant Nutrition) Regulations, 2020 | IMS Act, 1992 | Codex STAN 72-1981 (Infant Formula) | Codex STAN 156-1987 (Follow-Up Formula) | Codex STAN 074-1981 (Complementary Foods)

### Key Sub-Categories & Standards

| Sub-Category | Compositional Standard |
|---|---|
| Infant Formula (0–6 months) | Energy 60–70 kcal/100 mL; Protein 1.8–3.0 g/100 kcal; DHA mandatory (≥20 mg/100 kcal); Iron 0.3–1.3 mg/100 kcal; Vitamin D 1–2.5 μg/100 kcal; Vitamin A 60–180 μg RE/100 kcal |
| Follow-Up Formula (6–12 months) | Energy ≥60 kcal/100 mL; Protein 2.0–3.5 g/100 kcal; Iron 1.0–2.0 mg/100 kcal; Iodine mandatory; Zinc; higher Ca:P ratio than infant formula |
| Growing-Up Milk / Toddler Formula (1–3 yrs) | Fortified milk/formula — protein quality PDCAAS ≥0.7, micronutrient profile, DHA recommended |
| Processed Cereal-Based Foods (PCBF) | Minimum 25% protein from high-quality source; vitamin & mineral fortification per Schedule; moisture ≤5% |
| Complementary Foods & RUTF | Energy density ≥0.8 kcal/g; protein quality (PDCAAS); micronutrient profile aligned with WHO RUTF standards |
| IMS Act, 1992 Restrictions | No advertising of infant formula or follow-up formula to public; no free samples to mothers, healthcare workers, or general public; health warnings on pack |
| Prohibited Substances | No added honey, corn syrup, table salt, added sugars (sucrose/glucose/fructose as primary sugar) in infant formula; no artificial flavours; no colours |

**Key Identifiers:** IMS Act 1992 — No Public Advertising · Codex STAN 72/156 · Strict Compositional Limits · DHA Mandatory · No Added Sugars/Salt/Honey

## Category 10 — Novel Foods

**Definition:** Food or food ingredient with no significant history of safe consumption in India, or produced by a new/novel process not previously used for food production which could affect its safety or nutritional value. Includes new plant sources, insects, single-cell proteins, cell-based products, and foods from new processing technologies. Requires case-by-case safety evaluation and FSSAI approval before marketing.

**Regulation:** FSS (Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals, FSDU, FSMP, Functional Food & Novel Food) Regulations, 2022 — Schedule VII (Approved Novel Foods) | FSSAI Guidance on Novel Food Safety Assessment

### Key Sub-Categories & Standards

| Novel Food Type | Regulatory Status |
|---|---|
| New Plant Sources (Approved) | Chia seeds (Salvia hispanica), Quinoa, Hemp seeds/oil, Sacha Inchi — approved and listed in Schedule VII after safety review |
| Insect-Derived Foods | Currently novel — full FSSAI case-by-case safety evaluation required; no general approval yet |
| Single Cell Proteins & Algae | Spirulina (approved), Chlorella (approved), other microalgae — species-specific approval; heavy metal testing mandatory; daily dose limits |
| Cultured / Cell-Based Meat | Under regulatory development; FSSAI stakeholder consultation ongoing (2024–2025) |
| New Process-Derived Foods | HPP, Pulsed Electric Field (PEF), UV treatment — safety evaluation required even if base ingredient is conventional |
| Nanotechnology-Enabled Foods | Nano-encapsulated nutrients — novel; toxicokinetics and safety data required; no blanket approval |
| Novel Food Approval Process | Submit safety dossier (composition, toxicology, allergenicity, nutritional adequacy, exposure assessment, history of use elsewhere) → Expert Panel review → Schedule VII listing post-approval |

**Key Identifiers:** Case-by-Case FSSAI Approval · Safety Dossier Mandatory · Schedule VII Listing Post-Approval · No Prior History of Consumption · Expert Panel Review

## Category 11 — Non-Specified Foods

**Definition:** Foods or food ingredients not covered under any existing FSSAI category or standard, and which are neither proprietary foods nor novel foods in the formal regulatory sense. Must comply with general safety provisions of the FSS Act. FSSAI issues product-specific approvals. The residual regulatory category under Section 22 of the FSS Act.

**Regulation:** FSS Act, 2006 — Section 22 | FSS (Approval for Non-Specified Food & Food Ingredients) Regulations, 2017 | General Safety Provisions — Sections 3(zz) & 26

### Key Sub-Categories & Standards

| Standard Area | Details |
|---|---|
| General Safety Requirement | Must not be unsafe per Section 3(zz) of FSS Act — no injurious, harmful, or adulterated substances; must be fit for human consumption |
| Section 22 Approval Process | Apply to FSSAI Central Licensing Authority with full product details, composition, intended use, safety evidence — FSSAI grants product-specific approval certificate |
| GMP & Hygiene Standards | FSS (Licensing & Registration of Food Businesses) Regulations, 2011 — GMP, HACCP, sanitation standards apply even without compositional standard |
| Mandatory Labelling | Must comply fully with FSS (Labelling & Display) Regulations, 2020 — ingredient list, allergen declaration, nutritional information, batch no., date |
| Food Additive Permissions | No food additive permitted unless it appears in Schedule II for the closest applicable FCS category — no exemptions |
| Contaminant & Residue Limits | Appendix A contaminant limits and Appendix B pesticide residue limits apply universally — no exemption for non-specified foods |

**Key Identifiers:** Section 22 Approval · General FSS Act Compliance · No Specific Compositional Standard · FSSAI Case-by-Case · Full Labelling Compliance Required

## Classification Decision Logic

Use this logic when classifying a product:

```
1. Does a standard exist for this product in Schedule I?
   YES → Standard Food (Category 1)
   NO  → Continue

2. Is it a Standard Food with added nutrients + '+F' logo?
   YES → Fortified Food (Category 2)
   NO  → Continue

3. Is the product formulated for a specific disease/clinical condition requiring medical supervision?
   YES → FSMP (Category 7)
   NO  → Continue

4. Is it for a special dietary group (gluten-free, diabetic, sports, infant)?
   Infant (0–3 yrs) → Infant Nutrition (Category 9)
   Other group      → FSDU (Category 6)

5. Is it in dose form (tablet, capsule, powder) with nutrients/botanicals?
   Therapeutic / high-dose nutrients → Health Supplement (Category 4)
   Bioactives / functional ingredients → Nutraceutical (Category 5)

6. Does it contain defined live microorganisms or prebiotic ingredients?
   YES → Prebiotic/Probiotic Food (Category 8)
   NO  → Continue

7. Does the product have no history of safe consumption in India?
   YES → Novel Food (Category 10) — safety dossier + FSSAI approval required
   NO  → Continue

8. Is the product formulation unique, not standardised, but uses known ingredients?
   YES → Proprietary Food (Category 3) — prior FSSAI approval required
   NO  → Non-Specified Food (Category 11) — Section 22 approval required
```

## Additive Permission Rule (Critical)

> When any food additive is used in a product — regardless of category — its permission flows from the **Food Category System (FCS)** classification of that product under **Schedule II of FSS (Food Products Standards & Food Additives) Regulations, 2011**. The FCS category determines which additives, at what levels, are legally permitted.
